Discovering protein-protein interactions using single-molecule FRET and neural networks

K99 at NIGMS (submitted Oct 2022; not discussed)

Jozsef Meszaros, JD, PhD

In the laboratory of Jonathan Javitch

Science

How interactions among cell membrane proteins are mediated by and depend on physicochemical properties of proteins and presence of membrane components, initially examining synthetic protein segments and eventually including chemokine receptors

Techniques

Single-molecule imaging combined with simultaneous recording of fluorescence resonance energy transfer (smFRET) between pairs of molecules to collect data about protein movement through the membrane and interactions among proteins, to be analyzed using statiscal models and neural networks

Physicochemical properties of a protein

Liquid-ordered membrane microdomains

areas of the cell membrane enriched in sphingolipids and cholesterol

Aim 1

Generate cell lines to express transmembrane protein segments

  • Six model single-pass membrane proteins
  • Stable CHO cell lines expressing SNAP-tagged proteins
  • LEx-FITR CHO cells, which allow low expression under tetracycline control

Image isolated plasma membrane vesicles (PMVs) from the cell lines to measure protein localization into lipid domains

  • Live stain liquid-ordered and liquid-disordered areas of the membrane (rhoPE or DiO); image using a standard inverted epifluorescence microscope
  • Stain proteins using compatible dyes
  • Correlate protein dye with membrane dye

Total internal reflection microscopy imaging to perform smFRET

  • Dyes: Lumidyne 555 (LD555) and 655 (LD655)
  • Laser: a 532-nm (Torus 150 mW, Laser Quantum) and 640-nm (Cell Laser 100 mW, Olympus)
  • Camera: Evolve 512, Photometrics
  • Frame rate: 67 Hz (1 frame per 15 ms)
  • Imaging duration: 1 min
  • Image 4-5 cells per coverslip, two coverslips, 10 cells total
  • Only cell in focus is illuminated by evanescent TIRF field (not direct laser excitation)

Aim 1 (obstacles)

Diffusing too fast to separate diffusion states

Optimize system to enable faster imaging

Proteins not interacting leads to no FRET

Modify proteins by adding palmitoylation sites

Higher expression

Different proteins

Expected result: Proteins with hydrophobic residues and/or palmitoylation sites will partition into liquid-ordered areas of PMV; produce FRET events

Aim 2

Modifying membranes to alter distribution of liquid-ordered microdomains

  • 5mM MBCD loaded with 100nM cholesterol
  • 1mM deoxycholic acid
  • fewer smFRET detections when rafts are disrupted, more when rafts enhanced

Modifying proteins to alter propensity to enter lipid microdomains

  • adding or mutating cysteine residues where palmitoylation of a protein could occur
  • truncate transmembrane domains
  • protein modifications that decrease propensity for membrane microdomains will lead to fewer smFRET events

Total internal reflection microscopy imaging to perform smFRET

  • with either fluorescent cholesterol and/or membrane dyes to capture information about membrane composition dynamically
